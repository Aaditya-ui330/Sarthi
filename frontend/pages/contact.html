Sarthi is a compact, privacy-focused app that helps users get a quick sense of how safe different areas feel at different times. It combines short community impressions with contextual map signals so people can make more informed decisions while traveling.

I led the development of this web app as part of our mini-project, with help from my co-developers. Together we focused on making the interface clear and the experience lightweight so the app is easy to use on the go.

I’m a 3rd-year AIML engineering student at BMSIT with a strong interest in machine learning and crafting attractive, usable interfaces. Projects like this let me combine data, design, and engineering to build practical tools.
